Methodology Overview

GreenTrace Kenya implements the IPCC 2006 Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories updated with IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6, 2021) Global Warming Potential values. All calculations follow a Scope 1/2/3 framework aligned with the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard and are suitable for ISO 14064-1:2018 compliant reporting.

Emissions (tCO₂e) = Σ [Activity Data × Emission Factor × GWP₁₀₀] ÷ 1000
where GWP₁₀₀(CH₄) = 27.9, GWP₁₀₀(N₂O) = 273 (IPCC AR6 WG1 Ch.7)

Five emission sectors are covered: Borehole Operations, Livestock Management, Transport & Logistics, Construction & Infrastructure, and Manufacturing & SMEs. Kenya-specific emission factors are sourced from KPLC (2022), DEFRA/BEIS (2023), World Steel Association (2023), University of Bath ICE v3.0, and IPCC 2006 regional tables for Sub-Saharan Africa.

Borehole Methodology

Borehole emission calculations cover three scopes following GHG Protocol boundaries. Scope 1 captures direct diesel combustion during drilling and ongoing pumping operations. Scope 2 accounts for grid electricity used in electric pump systems. Scope 3 amortises the embodied carbon of infrastructure materials over the operational lifetime of the borehole.

Scope 1 = (L_drill ÷ lifetime + L_pump + L_gen) × EF_diesel
Scope 2 = kWh_grid × (1 − solar%) × EF_KPLC
Scope 3 = [(kg_steel×1.85 + kg_PVC×2.41 + kg_cement×0.83) ÷ lifetime]
         + km_transport × load_t × 0.20 ÷ lifetime
Total = (Scope1 + Scope2 + Scope3) ÷ 1000 [tCO₂e/year]

One-time construction emissions (drilling fuel, casing materials, transport) are amortised over the borehole design lifetime — typically 20–30 years for ASAL boreholes in Kenya. The solar fraction directly reduces Scope 2 by offsetting KPLC grid consumption. Diesel EF: IPCC 2006 Vol.2 Table 3.2.2. Grid EF: KPLC 2022.

Livestock Methodology

Livestock emissions are calculated using IPCC 2006 Volume 4 Chapter 10 Tier 1 emission factors for Sub-Saharan Africa. Enteric fermentation and manure management CH₄ are converted to CO₂ equivalents using IPCC AR6 GWP₁₀₀ = 27.9. Manure N₂O uses IPCC Tier 1 default manure management system fractions for pasture-based systems — the dominant system in Kenya's ASAL regions.

E_enteric = Σ(heads × EF_enteric) × GWP_CH₄
E_manure_CH₄ = Σ(heads × EF_manure_CH₄) × GWP_CH₄
E_manure_N₂O = Σ(heads × Nex × EF3_pasture × 44/28) × GWP_N₂O
E_feed_S3 = kg_concentrate × 0.45 kgCO₂e/kg
E_energy_S2 = kWh × EF_KPLC
Total = (E_enteric + E_manure_CH₄ + E_manure_N₂O + E_feed_S3 + E_energy_S2) ÷ 1000

Transport & Logistics Methodology

Transport emissions follow DEFRA/BEIS 2023 emission factor guidelines. GreenTrace supports fuel-based and distance-based activity data methods. The fuel-based method is recommended when fuel purchase records are available (e.g. fleet fuel cards, SACCO fuel logs). The distance-based method suits operators tracking odometer data.

Fuel-based: E = Σ(L_fuel × EF_fuel) ÷ 1000
Distance-based: E = Σ(km × EF_km_or_pax) ÷ 1000
Refrigerant: E_ref = kg_HFC134a × 1430 + kg_R404A × 3922 (÷ 1000 for t)
Rail freight: E_rail = tonne·km × 0.028 kgCO₂e/tonne·km
Marine diesel: E_marine = L_diesel × EF_diesel
Total = E_fuel_or_dist + E_ref + E_rail + E_marine [tCO₂e/year]

Construction & Infrastructure Methodology

Construction emissions are dominated by Scope 3 embodied carbon — the carbon locked into building materials during their manufacture. These are calculated using University of Bath ICE v3.0 cradle-to-gate emission factors, which are widely used for East African construction projects. Scope 1 covers on-site machinery diesel. Scope 2 covers site office and lighting electricity.

Scope 3 (embodied) = Σ(quantity_material × EF_material)
  [e.g. tonnes_cement × 830 kgCO₂e/t + m³_concrete × 159 kgCO₂e/m³ + ...]
Scope 1 (site diesel) = (L_excavator + L_mixer + L_gen) × months × EF_diesel
                  + truck·km × EF_truck
Scope 2 (electricity) = kWh_month × months × EF_KPLC
Total = (Scope1 + Scope2 + Scope3) ÷ 1000 [tCO₂e, project lifetime]

Manufacturing & SMEs Methodology

Manufacturing emissions follow IPCC 2006 Volume 3 (Industrial Processes) and Volume 2 (Energy) guidelines. The three-scope framework identifies direct process combustion (Scope 1), purchased electricity (Scope 2), and refrigerant fugitive emissions plus waste (Scope 3). An emission intensity metric — kgCO₂e per tonne of product — enables benchmarking against sector peers and international standards.

Scope 1 = (L_HFO×3.17 + L_diesel×2.68 + kg_LPG×2.98) + tonnes_process_CO₂×1000
         + tonnes_biomass × 112 kgCO₂e/GJ × 16 GJ/t (non-sustainable)
Scope 2 = (kWh_grid − kWh_solar) × EF_KPLC
Scope 3 = kg_HFC134a×1430 + kg_R404A×3922
         + m³_wastewater×25 + tonnes_landfill×580
Intensity = Total_kgCO₂e ÷ tonnes_product [kgCO₂e/tonne]
Total = (Scope1 + Scope2 + Scope3) ÷ 1000 [tCO₂e/year]

GreenTrace Score Formula

The GreenTrace Score (GS Score, 0–100) is a composite performance index combining emission intensity, offset ratio, year-on-year reduction, and platform engagement. It allows organisations of different sizes and sectors to be benchmarked on a common scale.

Intensity_Score = 40 × max(0, 1 − E_intensity / sector_benchmark)
Offset_Score = 30 × min(1, total_offsets / total_emissions)
Reduction_Score = 20 × max(0, annual_reduction_rate / 0.07)
Engagement_Score = 10 × min(1, projects_tracked / 10)
GS_Score = Σ(components) [0–100, rounded to integer]
ScoreGradeStatusDescription
85–100A+🌟 Climate LeaderSector-leading intensity, active offsetting, consistent reduction
70–84A🌿 Climate PositiveBelow-benchmark intensity, measurable offset programme
55–69B📈 ImprovingTracking emissions, some offset activity, year-on-year decline
40–54C⚠️ Needs AttentionAbove-benchmark intensity, limited offsets, slow reduction
0–39D🔴 High EmitterHigh intensity, no offsets, no reduction trajectory

The 7% annual reduction target aligns with the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) 1.5°C pathway. Organisations achieving a 7% or greater reduction score full marks on the Reduction component.

🌍 Our Mission

GreenTrace Kenya exists to give smallholder farmers, county governments, and agricultural businesses the tools to measure, understand, and reduce their carbon footprint — without needing a PhD in climate science.

By combining IPCC AR6 methodology with Kenya-calibrated emission factors, we provide auditable emission data that meets the standards of international carbon markets, donor reporting, and national climate commitments (Kenya NDC 2030).

🎯 Why GreenTrace?

Agriculture contributes over 33% of Kenya's total greenhouse gas emissions, yet most farmers have no way to quantify their impact. GreenTrace fills this gap — providing sector-specific calculators built on peer-reviewed science, not approximations.

Our platform generates ISO 14064-aligned reports accepted by county governments, UN agencies, World Bank-funded programmes, and voluntary carbon markets including Verra VCS and Gold Standard.

KNCR Registration Process — Step by Step
📝
Step 1 — Project Concept
Submit a Project Concept Note (PCN) to DNA (NEMA). GreenTrace generates this automatically from your calculation data.
📋
Step 2 — Project Design Document
Develop a full PDD under Verra VCS, Gold Standard, or domestic KNCR methodology. GreenTrace exports IPCC-compliant data for your verifier.
🔍
Step 3 — Third-Party Validation
Engage an accredited Validation/Verification Body (VVB). All GreenTrace reports are formatted for auditor hand-off.
🏛️
Step 4 — DNA Review & Approval
NEMA / Climate Change Directorate reviews against Kenya's NDC and issues a Letter of Authorisation (LoA) for Article 6 ITMOs.
📲
Step 5 — KNCR Registration
Project listed on kncr.go.ke with public project page. All documents uploaded to KNCR portal. GreenTrace data auto-populates emission fields.
🏅
Step 6 — Credit Issuance
Verified credits issued on KNCR. 25% community share transferred. ITMOs available for bilateral agreements (Switzerland, Sweden, Singapore, South Korea).
